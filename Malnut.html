What is Malnutrition?

Malnutrition is a condition that arises when a person's body does not receive enough nutrients, vitamins, and minerals required for normal growth, development, and maintenance of healthy body tissues and functions. Malnutrition can either result from undernutrition, where a person is not getting enough nutrients, or overnutrition, where a person is consuming too many calories or nutrients but not in the right proportions.

Malnutrition can have a range of health consequences, including weakened immune systems, stunted growth, impaired cognitive development, and an increased risk of disease and death. It is a significant problem worldwide, affecting millions of people, particularly in low-income and developing countries.
